Banana Pancakes

Fluffy on the inside, crispy on the outside, and delicately flavored with bananas and vanilla—these are phenomenal banana pancakes. The recipe, believe it or not, is adapted from a Williams Sonoma children’s cookbook, which only proves how easy they are to make. I love them with a simple drizzle of maple syrup, but if you want to dress them up and hint at what’s inside, top them with fresh sliced bananas and a dusting of confectioners’ sugar.

prep time

15 min.

cook time

30 min.

serving

4 servings

Tags : pancake breakfast

instructions

step 1

Gather all ingredients.

step 2

Combine flour, white sugar, baking powder, and salt in a bowl. Mix together egg, milk, vegetable oil, and bananas in a second bowl.

step 3

Stir flour mixture into banana mixture; batter will be slightly lumpy.

step 4

Heat a lightly oiled griddle or frying pan over medium high heat. Pour or scoop the batter onto the griddle, using approximately 1/4 cup for each pancake.

step 5

Cook until pancakes are golden brown, 3 to 5 minutes per side. Serve hot.

step 5

Serve hot and enjoy!

ingredients

1/2 cup Whole Wheat Flour / All Purpose Flour

1/2 teaspoon Baking Powde

1 teaspoon Sugar and A Pinch of Salt

1/4 cup mashed Ripe Banana (approx. 1 medium)

1/2 cup Milk

1 tablespoon Curd and 2-3 drops of Vanilla Extract

1 tablespoon Butter + for greasing and Maple Syrup, for serving

1 tablespoon Chopped Cashew nuts or Almonds (for serving) optional

tools

Hand Blender

Large Heavy Pot With Lid

Measuring Spoons

Measuring Cups
